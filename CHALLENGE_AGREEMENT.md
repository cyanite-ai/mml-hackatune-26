Acceptance of this Agreement is recorded through the Organizer's (Munich Music Labs e.V.) registration process before a participant begins work on the Cyanite challenge, and constitutes a binding agreement directly between the participant and Cyanite.

Participation in the challenge does not grant participants any ownership rights in Cyanite intellectual property.

Participants acknowledge that Cyanite is actively conducting internal research and product development in areas related to music understanding, conversational search, multimodal retrieval, recommendation systems, and related technologies.

Participants are responsible for ensuring that any content, datasets, media, or third-party tools used in their projects comply with applicable laws and licensing terms, including the Creative Commons license terms of any track provided.

Participants may not attempt to:

- extract proprietary model data,
- reproduce embeddings or model outputs at scale,
- bulk-download, mirror, or otherwise systematically harvest the dataset or its analysis,
- circumvent usage limitations or rate limits,
- or misuse provided infrastructure.

5. Data Protection

The provided challenge data may include limited information relating to individual users of third-party platforms (for example, user identifiers and their associated track preferences). Where such data is provided, Cyanite provides it in pseudonymized form.

Participants agree to:
- process the provided data solely for the purpose of participating in the Cyanite challenge at HACKATUNE 2026,
- not attempt to re-identify any individual, or combine the data with other sources to do so,
- not retain or further distribute the data beyond what is necessary for the Event,
- and comply with applicable data protection law, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Participants acknowledge that submitted projects may be publicly presented, demonstrated, or showcased by Munich Music Labs e.V. and Cyanite in connection with HACKATUNE 2026 and related promotional activities, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Ownership of participant projects remains unaffected by such showcase rights.

Unless explicitly designated otherwise in writing, no information provided by Cyanite as part of the challenge shall be considered confidential, except for API credentials and Cyanite Outputs, which must not be published or redistributed and must be deleted after the Event as set out in Section 2.
